ABOUT

A small hobby project that helps parents find the right playground or park — not just a park, but one that's fenced, shaded, has a bathroom, and is actually toddler-friendly.

The problem

Map apps tell you a park exists. They don't tell you the things a parent of a little kid actually needs to know before loading everyone into the car: Is it fenced so a toddler can't bolt? Is there shade in July? A bathroom for the inevitable emergency? Somewhere to park? That information is buried in hundreds of reviews nobody has time to read. So you drive over, and half the time it's a dud.

How it works

The app reads the reviews for you. Here's the pipeline behind every search:

  1. Find the parks. The Google Places API returns nearby playgrounds and parks, along with their photos, ratings, hours, and recent reviews.
  2. Read the reviews with AI. Each park's reviews are sent to Google's Gemini model, which extracts structured answers to six specific questions (fenced? shade? bathrooms? toddler-friendly? parking? tennis courts?) and writes a one-line summary for each.
  3. Cross-check against the map. The same parks are looked up in OpenStreetMap, whose community has tagged real-world features — fences, restrooms, parking lots, splash pads, picnic tables, drinking fountains. Where the map confirms a feature, the app marks it verified.
  4. Layer in Google's own facts. Google publishes structured fields for some parks (has a restroom, good for children); those are treated as first-party truth and take precedence.

That's why each feature shows one of two badges: a ✓ from reviews (Gemini's read of what visitors said), or a ✓ verified (confirmed by map or Google data). Reviews are fast and rich; map data is authoritative. Showing both, honestly labeled, is the whole point.

The six things that matter

🔒 Fenced 🌳 Shade 🚻 Bathrooms 👶 Toddler-friendly 🅿️ Parking 🎾 Tennis

Plus weather for the search area, an estimated drive time for nap-window planning, a way to save favorites and private notes, and one-tap sharing of a park or a whole collection.

Built with

  • Frontend: hand-written HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — no framework. Installable as a PWA with offline support.
  • Backend: Vercel serverless functions (Node) that keep the API keys server-side.
  • AI: Google Gemini (2.5 Flash Lite) for review analysis.
  • Data: Google Places API, OpenStreetMap (Overpass), Open-Meteo for weather, Nominatim for address search.
  • Maps: Leaflet with OpenStreetMap tiles.

Privacy

There are no accounts and no tracking. Your favorites, notes, recent searches, and home location live only in your own browser — nothing is sent to a server or shared. Clearing your browser data clears them.

Made by AWW as a personal project — free to use, no ads. If a feature looks wrong for a specific park, it's usually because the underlying reviews or map data are sparse there.
